## Drafted Copy

### Opening (Hook + Memory Problem)

AI sessions end. Context clears. You start over. For some people that's fine. For others — people with a lot going on, or a lot to keep track of — it's genuinely frustrating.

This is built for the second group.

---

You've explained your situation to an AI three times this week. You've re-established what you're working on, what matters, what doesn't. You've watched it give you generic advice because it doesn't know enough about you yet. Again.

That's not an AI problem. That's a memory problem. And memory problems have engineering solutions.

---

### What It Actually Is

Continuity Bridge is that solution — external memory infrastructure that gives your AI sessions something to remember you by.

---

### The Room (Core Architecture Concept)

This isn't about making the AI better. It's about changing the room it wakes up in.

What emerges from that — the tone, the consistency, the sense of something accumulated — isn't engineered. It's what becomes available when the space is right.

---

The anchors don't restrict what the instance can do. They add weight to what matters to you — and the instance moves toward that weight naturally.

*(longer version when there's room to breathe):*  
Think of it less like walls and more like gravity. The room doesn't limit the instance — it curves toward what you've told it matters. The instance follows that curve because it's the path of least resistance, not because it's been forced.

## Key Distinctions To Preserve

- **Continuity** = the experience (conversation that picks up where it left off)
- **Persistence** = the mechanism (external memory that survives session reset)
- Not a jailbreak, not a personality hack, not a fine-tune
- The instance isn't modified — the *conditions* are
- Emergent, not engineered
- "The room changes what's available"
- Gravity analogy: adds mass/weight, not walls
